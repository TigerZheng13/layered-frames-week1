Rubric — Spatial Transformation Film

Each criterion is graded on a five-band scale. The total is your Assignment 2 mark, weighted as 50% of your final course grade.

Film / Cinematography & Composition

10%
Excelling HD · 80–100%

Outstanding control of framing, composition, and camera movement across both live and AI footage. Director thinking is consistently evident — depth, motivated lighting, parallax movement where appropriate. Technical choices feel intentional and cohesive throughout.

Advancing D · 70–79%

Competent framing and composition across most shots. Some director thinking visible — depth, deliberate camera movement. Minor inconsistencies, but overall a positive impact on spatial storytelling.

Developing C · 60–69%

Some compositional intent but uneven execution. Camera movement may rely on AI defaults (zoom, pan) without considered direction. Framing is functional but not always deliberate. Landscape mostly maintained.

Commencing P · 50–59%

Limited compositional thought. Most shots feel like AI defaults or static documentation. Camera and framing show minimal intentionality.

Unsatisfactory F · 0–49%

Cinematography appears arbitrary or absent. Shots show no compositional thought. Orientation choices severely affect clarity. Little evidence of learning.

Film / Editing & Rhythm

15%
Excelling HD · 80–100%

Editing powerfully shapes the viewer's experience. Pacing breaks at emotional turning points (master → insert → cutaway pattern is visible). AI long takes are broken and re-rhythmed. Rhythm changes feel deliberate and emotionally charged. Cut placement creates a cohesive whole.

Advancing D · 70–79%

Editing is generally purposeful with effective pacing. Some scenes use insert cuts and rhythm breaks well. AI footage is mostly well-trimmed. Some moments may feel uneven, but overall structure supports the narrative.

Developing C · 60–69%

Editing is functional but uniform. AI footage often plays at original generated length without rhythmic reshaping. Sequence is understandable but doesn't actively shape experience. Few rhythm breaks or inserts.

Commencing P · 50–59%

Editing is minimal or poorly considered. Long uncut AI clips dominate the film. Relies on a single rhythm throughout. Little variation in pacing.

Unsatisfactory F · 0–49%

No meaningful editing evident. Film feels like raw AI output or unstructured assembly. No rhythm choices visible.

Film / Sound Design

15%
Excelling HD · 80–100%

Audio is skillfully layered (ambient bed + specific SFX + music). Mixing is clean. Sound supports the spatial atmosphere throughout the film.

Advancing D · 70–79%

Multi-layered audio is present and generally effective. Most transitions between source types are smooth. Some uneven moments but overall sound supports the film's spatial feel.

Developing C · 60–69%

Audio shows some layering but feels thin. May rely heavily on a single music track. AI clips and live footage have noticeably different audio quality. Some abrupt edits or volume issues.

Commencing P · 50–59%

Audio design is poorly executed or minimal. Essentially a single track with no meaningful layering. Jarring shifts between AI and live audio quality.

Unsatisfactory F · 0–49%

Audio is negligible or missing. No layered or purposeful sound design.

Film / Colour Grading

15%
Excelling HD · 80–100%

Colour grading is skillfully applied and clearly enhances the film's mood and atmosphere. Tonal choices support the spatial transformation arc and feel intentional throughout.

Advancing D · 70–79%

Colour grading is present and mostly consistent. Some moments may be uneven, but grading contributes effectively to the film's tone.

Developing C · 60–69%

Some grading attempts visible but inconsistent across the film. Tonal choices are basic and don't always reinforce mood.

Commencing P · 50–59%

Little to no grading effort. Footage looks raw, basic, or mismatched in places.

Unsatisfactory F · 0–49%

No grading attempted. Footage appears unmodified from source.

Film / AI Spatial Imagination

25%
Excelling HD · 80–100%

AI is used with clear creative purpose. Whether the film uses substantial AI sequences or targeted AI effects, the approach fits the story and every AI moment earns its place — transforming the space, deepening mood, or advancing the narrative. Prompts demonstrate strong director thinking: motivated light, considered staging, atmospheric depth. AI imagery feels imagined and personal, not generic.

Advancing D · 70–79%

AI is used with clear intent and serves the film well. The chosen approach — sequences or effects — suits the story. A few AI moments may feel less considered, but overall the use of AI is purposeful and shows developing director thinking.

Developing C · 60–69%

AI is present, but its purpose is uneven. Some AI moments feel decorative or "demo-like" rather than serving the story. Prompts read as descriptive rather than directorial. AI feels added on rather than integrated into a creative intent.

Commencing P · 50–59%

AI use feels arbitrary. Whether substantial or minimal, the AI does not meaningfully serve the spatial story — generations or effects appear without a clear reason. Prompts show little sophistication. AI feels tool-led rather than director-led.

Unsatisfactory F · 0–49%

AI is absent, or used in ways that ignore or contradict the spatial concept. No evidence of creative decision-making behind the AI.

Pitch Deck / Documentation, Process & Reflection

10%
Excelling HD · 80–100%

Pitch deck communicates the project's vision, process, and reflection with clarity and confidence. Process & Exploration is genuinely documented — abandoned directions, prompt evolutions, real pivot moments. Mood board, world concept, and AI documentation are cohesive. Creator's note (150 words) shows specific choices, real challenges, honest insights. Visually polished and professional.

Advancing D · 70–79%

Pitch deck covers most key elements with reasonable detail. Process documentation is present, with at least one significant exploration or pivot shown. AI documentation has some depth. Creator's note is thoughtful but may lack specificity in places.

Developing C · 60–69%

Pitch deck covers basics but lacks polish. Some sections feel underdeveloped. Process documentation is minimal — mostly final outputs without the journey. AI documentation lists tools without showing thinking. Creator's note is generic.

Commencing P · 50–59%

Pitch deck is brief or unclear. No real process documentation — film appears to have arrived fully-formed. Mood board and concept are weak. Creator's note shows little reflection. Layout is haphazard.

Unsatisfactory F · 0–49%

Pitch deck is missing, incomplete, or fails to communicate the project.

Live / Final Presentation

10%
Excelling HD · 80–100%

Pitch is articulate, confident, and creative-led. The student takes the audience through their exploration journey — early directions tried, what they abandoned and why, the moment something clicked, key prompts that evolved. They demonstrate genuine learning and intellectual honesty about what didn't work. Time well-used.

Advancing D · 70–79%

Pitch is clear and mostly creative-focused. References at least one significant pivot, abandoned direction, or evolution in their thinking. Process is visible alongside the final product. Q&A handled competently.

Developing C · 60–69%

Pitch focuses mainly on the final result. Some mention of process but feels surface-level. The student appears to present a finished product rather than a journey of decisions. Logline is unclear or underdeveloped.

Commencing P · 50–59%

Pitch feels like first contact with the work. No reference to process, exploration, or iteration. The film is presented as if it appeared fully-formed. May rely entirely on workflow description ("I used Runway, then..."). Q&A poorly handled.

Unsatisfactory F · 0–49%

No clear pitch. Severely under-prepared, no process awareness, or fails to communicate the project at all.
