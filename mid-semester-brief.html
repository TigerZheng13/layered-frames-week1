Mid-Semester Brief

Explore the technical and creative aspects of spatial narrative filmmaking, focusing on thoughtful use of camera, sound, editing, and colour grading to communicate space through moving image.

You are not required to create a strictly narrative or linear film. Your project may be abstract, experimental, or narrative-driven as you prefer.

Part 1

Short film (2–3 minutes)

Submit a refined and cohesive short film that clearly demonstrates the filmmaking techniques you've explored over the past weeks.

Cinematography

  • Shot primarily in landscape (horizontal) orientation. Portrait clips allowed as intentional inserts only.
  • Deliberate use of camera techniques: pan, tilt, handheld, static, tracking shots.
  • Consideration of framing and composition to support your narrative or spatial concept.

Editing

  • Clear and purposeful editing choices that shape how the viewer experiences space and time.
  • Avoid overly simple editing (such as a single continuous music track over uncut footage).
  • Demonstrate intentional, varied cuts and pacing.

Sound design

  • Thoughtful layered audio tracks enhancing your visual narrative.
  • Effective use of ambient sounds, effects, transitions, and music.

Colour grading

  • Purposeful colour grading choices that enhance mood and storytelling.

Lighting optional

  • Awareness of how natural and artificial light shapes the spaces you film.
  • Experimentation with lighting direction, quality, or colour covered in class.

AI tools optional

  • You may incorporate AI-generated or AI-enhanced elements (e.g., Runway, Kling).
  • If used, briefly note what tools and how they contributed in your pitch deck.

Quality expectations: Your film should demonstrate genuine spatial exploration, not something shot in 10 minutes in a single spot. We expect variation in locations or spatial perspectives, a range of camera movements, and layered sound design with multiple audio tracks.

Solo work & footage sharing

  • This is an individual assignment. Every student must submit their own film.
  • You may share up to approximately 1 minute of raw footage from Week 1 group filming with classmates.
  • The remaining footage must be your own. Your editing, sound design, and colour grading must be entirely individual.
  • Identical or near-identical edits will not be accepted.
Part 2

Pitch deck

Prepare a concise presentation that supports your film. You will present it in person during class in Week 6.

Title & logline

A concise summary capturing your project's essence.

Mood board

Visual references establishing tone, style, and colour palette.

Story / concept

Outline of your narrative or conceptual framework.

Location & characters

Briefly outline locations and characters involved, if relevant.

Technical choices

Highlight notable techniques: camera movements, colour grading approach, sound design, AI tools if applicable.

Reflection

Discuss challenges encountered during production and your solutions.

References or inspirations optional

Films, media, or spaces that influenced your work.

Cinematography & lighting 20%

HD 80–100

Outstanding control of camera movement, framing, and composition across varied spatial settings. Landscape orientation used consistently. Lighting awareness clearly enhances the spatial narrative.
D 70–79

Competent camera handling across more than one spatial setting, with some creative lighting awareness. Minor inconsistencies.
C 60–69

Some spatial variation but may feel repetitive or limited to one location. Lighting awareness is basic or unevenly applied.
P 50–59

Limited choices with little spatial variation. May rely heavily on portrait orientation. Minimal intentionality.
F 0–49

Minimal or absent. No meaningful spatial exploration. Little evidence of learning.

Editing 20%

HD 80–100

Editing choices powerfully shape the viewer's experience of space and time. Pacing, rhythm, and cut placement are deliberate.
D 70–79

Generally purposeful with effective pacing. Some moments may feel uneven, but overall supports the narrative.
C 60–69

Functional but lacks variety or intentionality. Does not actively enhance the spatial experience.
P 50–59

Minimal or poorly considered. Relies on overly simple approaches with little variation.
F 0–49

No meaningful editing evident. Footage appears unstructured or assembled without purpose.

Sound design 20%

HD 80–100

Skillfully layered across multiple tracks. Ambient sound, effects, and music reinforce mood and spatial atmosphere. Mixing is clean.
D 70–79

Multiple tracks and generally effective, though some uneven mixing or abrupt transitions may appear.
C 60–69

Some layering but lacks depth. May rely too heavily on a single music track.
P 50–59

Poorly executed or minimal. Essentially a single audio track with no meaningful layering.
F 0–49

Negligible or missing. No clear attempt at layered sound design.

Colour grading 15%

HD 80–100

Greatly enhances mood and spatial atmosphere; skillfully applied and consistent throughout.
D 70–79

Present and mostly consistent; minor mismatches. Contributes to the film's tone.
C 60–69

Minimal or inconsistent. Some attempts, but outcomes vary in quality.
P 50–59

Little to no grading, or very poor application. Images often look raw or mismatched.
F 0–49

Virtually no effort; content remains unmodified from original source.

Mood board & visual identity 10%

HD 80–100

Strongly aligns with the project's intended tone, colours, and spatial themes. Cohesive and highly indicative of the final vision.
D 70–79

Cohesive and consistent, generally reflecting the project's aesthetic. Minor gaps.
C 60–69

Uneven or loosely tied to the project's style. Some relevant images but lacks coherence.
P 50–59

Minimal effort; visuals may not represent the stated theme or tone.
F 0–49

Missing or unclear, fails to communicate any visual direction.

Key project info & reflection 10%

HD 80–100

Clearly outlines story/concept, locations, and key technical choices. Reflection is thoughtful, identifying specific challenges and solutions.
D 70–79

Outlines most key elements. Core idea and reflection are understandable.
C 60–69

Basic outline but missing clarity in key areas. Reflection is superficial or generic.
P 50–59

Very brief or unclear. Reflection is minimal, lacking specific insights.
F 0–49

No meaningful information about narrative or technical approach. No evidence of reflection.

Organisation & cohesion 5%

HD 80–100

Well-structured, flows logically, presented in a professional, visually appealing manner.
D 70–79

Organised and understandable, though minor layout or flow issues may appear.
C 60–69

Some structure present, but layout or flow feels fragmented.
P 50–59

Difficult to follow; information presented haphazardly or lacking clear flow.
F 0–49

Disorganised or incomplete, hard to identify key points.
